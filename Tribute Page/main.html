Bill Reid: A Timeline

  • 1920 William Ronald Reid Jr. is born in Victoria, British Columbia, to a Haida Canadian mother and Scottish-American father
  • 1939 Begins work as a radio announcer. He is so far unaware of his Haida heritage, due to the oppressive Indian Act
  • 1943 Visits Haida Gwaii for the first time as an adult, and begins to learn of his family history
  • 1944 Marries Mable van Boyen
  • 1944 Conscripted into the army for a year
  • 1948 Moves to Toronto to study jewlery at Ryerson Institute of Technology
  • 1951 Returns to British Columbia in moving to Vancouver
  • 1954 On a visit back to Haida Gwaii Bill discovers a bracelet carved by his great-great uncle, Charles Edenshaw, engraved in traditional Haida style. This style had been largely eroded since European colonization. Bill becomes obsessed with Haida style and begins to research pre-colonial Haida art
  • 1959 Bill and Mabel divorce, having had a daughter (Amanda) and adopting a son (Raymond)
  • 1968 Moves to London, England to study at the Central School of Art and Design
  • 1973 Having lived in Montreal and London, Bill returns to Vancouver following his Parkinsons diagnosis
  • 1980 Begins producing sculptures for the University of British Columbia Museum of Anthropology, including the Haida Village and The Raven and the First People
  • 1981 Marries Martine de Widerspach-Thor, and they participate protests to save rainforrests of Gwaii Haanas
  • 1998 Reid dies on March 13th, having recieved the National Aboriginal Achievement Award, the Order of British Columbia, and numerous other awards.
